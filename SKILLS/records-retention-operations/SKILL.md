# Records Retention Operations

## Overview

Organize records retention work as an operational workflow. Identify record classes, locations, retention authority, legal-hold flags, privacy constraints, disposition steps, audit evidence, and approval gates.

## Workflow

1. Restate the records scope, business owner, jurisdiction or policy source, system locations, and intended disposition or review action.
2. Inventory record classes, formats, systems, owners, date ranges, retention triggers, and related policies or schedules.
3. Map retention authority, privacy constraints, audit requirements, legal-hold indicators, and disposition blockers, then distinguish master records from duplicates, exports, or derivatives.
4. Define operational next steps for retention, preservation, disposition, review, or escalation, including the specific approval needed to proceed.
5. Produce approval gates and questions for counsel, records management, privacy, security, or business owners, plus an audit trail of what decision is pending.

Use `references/retention-workflow-checklist.md` for retention workflow checks.

## Output Contract

Return exactly: `Records Scope`, `Inventory`, `Retention Authority`, `Hold Or Privacy Flags`, `Disposition Workflow`, `Approval Gates`, `Owner Questions`, `Audit Notes`.

## Stop Conditions

Stop when asked to destroy, preserve, disclose, or classify records without authorized approval; when litigation hold, regulatory investigation, privacy restriction, or retention authority is unclear; or when legal advice is required.
