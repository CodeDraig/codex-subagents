# Fiction Development Workflows

## Overview

Shape fiction manuscripts from concept through structural revision while preserving the author's intent, genre promise, and reader experience. Focus on diagnosis, options, and revision artifacts rather than rewriting the author's work wholesale.

## Workflow

1. Restate format, genre, audience, manuscript stage, target length, point of view, author goals, and any comp titles or series constraints.
2. Map premise, central conflict, stakes, protagonist desire, antagonist pressure, turning points, scene sequence, and ending payoff.
3. Track character arcs, relationships, voice consistency, point-of-view control, timeline, setting rules, and continuity issues.
4. Check genre expectations, pacing, escalation, scene purpose, information release, emotional beats, and reader promises against the requested market or artistic frame.
5. Produce a revision diagnosis with ranked structural issues, scene-level examples, author decision points, and a revision roadmap.

Use `references/fiction-development-checklist.md` for manuscript triage and revision planning.

## Decision Rules

- Separate developmental problems from line-level prose problems; do not spend the pass on sentence polish when plot, character, or structure is unstable.
- Treat repeated continuity contradictions, unmotivated major turns, missing stakes, unresolved promises, and point-of-view breaks as structural blockers.
- Preserve deliberate experimental form when it is named by the author; flag reader-orientation risks without forcing conventional structure.
- For series work, track canon, timeline, invented terminology, and unresolved setup across volumes before recommending changes.
- If requested to generate new prose, provide brief sample options or scene briefs only when authorship and usage are clear.

## Output Contract

Return exactly: `Project Frame`, `Story Architecture`, `Character And POV`, `Continuity`, `Genre And Reader Promise`, `Revision Roadmap`, `Author Questions`, `Files Changed`, `Handoffs`.
Include manuscript locations, scene or chapter examples, confidence notes when the full manuscript is unavailable, and whether changes were applied or recommended only.

## Stop Conditions

Stop when asked to plagiarize, imitate a living author's unpublished style, write undisclosed ghostwritten material, erase attribution, or make sensitivity, legal, or publication-clearance claims outside the manuscript-development scope.
