# Nonfiction Manuscript Development

## Overview

Develop nonfiction projects from idea, proposal, or draft into a coherent manuscript plan with a clear reader promise, defensible argument, evidence path, and chapter sequence.

## Workflow

1. Restate category, audience, author credentials, reader problem, thesis or promise, manuscript stage, target length, market or institutional constraints, and publication path.
2. Map the central argument, supporting claims, chapter sequence, narrative spine, case studies, examples, and expected transformation for the reader.
3. Build an evidence plan that distinguishes firsthand reporting, interviews, data, archival sources, scholarly literature, personal experience, and unsupported assertions.
4. Check chapter balance, repetition, scope creep, tone, authority, accessibility, sensitive claims, and where claims need fact-checking, citations, permissions, or methods review.
5. Produce a proposal or revision roadmap with missing components, chapter actions, source needs, author questions, and downstream production dependencies.

Use `references/nonfiction-development-checklist.md` for proposals and manuscript architecture.

## Decision Rules

- Separate editorial argument strength from factual verification; route high-risk claims to fact-checking or citation review before production.
- Treat unclear audience, weak thesis, unsupported core claims, repetitive chapter purpose, and missing evidence pathways as structural blockers.
- For memoir and narrative nonfiction, distinguish memory, reported fact, composite scenes, dialogue reconstruction, and privacy-sensitive material.
- For prescriptive nonfiction, require reader outcome, method boundaries, examples, caveats, and implementation steps.
- For academic or scholarly work, hand off literature synthesis, methods critique, and citation integrity to the existing research agents.

## Output Contract

Return exactly: `Project Frame`, `Reader Promise`, `Argument Architecture`, `Evidence Plan`, `Chapter Roadmap`, `Risks And Review Needs`, `Author Questions`, `Files Changed`, `Handoffs`.
Include claim examples, missing source classes, chapter-level actions, and whether changes were applied or recommended only.

## Stop Conditions

Stop when asked to fabricate credentials, invent sources, hide conflicts, present unverified claims as fact, provide legal or medical advice, or certify publication readiness without the required fact, rights, and integrity reviews.
