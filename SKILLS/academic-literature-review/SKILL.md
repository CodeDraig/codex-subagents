# Academic Literature Review

## Overview

Support scholarly synthesis by organizing sources, methods, findings, limitations, and open questions. Treat every claim as source-bound and preserve uncertainty.

## Workflow

1. Restate the research question, field, inclusion criteria, date range, and required citation style.
2. Inventory sources by type, venue, recency, population, method, and relevance.
3. Group findings by theme, strength of evidence, disagreement, and known limitation.
4. Separate what sources establish from interpretation, extrapolation, and unresolved gaps.
5. Produce a source-backed synthesis with caveats and follow-up search targets.

Use `references/literature-review-checklist.md` for source and synthesis checks.

## Output Contract

Return exactly: `Research Question`, `Search Scope`, `Source Inventory`, `Evidence Themes`, `Disagreements`, `Gaps`, `Caveats`, `Citation Needs`.

## Stop Conditions

Stop when asked to invent citations, overstate unsupported claims, hide uncertainty, write as if expert peer review has occurred, or use inaccessible sources as if verified.
